A word on origins . . .

The Beaver family came to America some time in the mid-1700s. The first verifiable Beaver ancestor in the Americas was James Caleb Beaver, whose father (James Richard Beaver) lived and died in England and was buried at Cripplesgate Chapel in London, and whose son (Martin Caleb Beaver) fought in the American Revolution. The name, however, had already existed for many centuries, and has a variety of potential origins.

It may have been a nickname referring to a hard worker, or to someone who was believed to bare a resemblance to the animal, derived from the Middle English word "bevere" or the Old English (pre-7th century) word "beofor", both meaning "beaver". For those who are hesitant to make a connection to some form of nobility which cannot be verified, this may be the preferred origin. Most people before the Industrial Revolution were farmers and laborers with little wealth or social mobility.

Alternatively, the name may be of Norman origin. This applies to variants of the name including Beaver, Beever, Beevor, Bevar, Bever, Bevir, Biever and others. In this case, it derives from a number of Norman location names in France called Beauvoir or Belvoir, in areas of Normandy such as Somme, Manche, and Seine-Maritime. This variant of the name derives from the Old French words "beu, bel" (meaning "fair" or "lovely") and "veir, voir" (meaning "to see" or "view"), or "a place with a nice view". These very earliest of Beaver ancestors from Normandy were granted lands in Berkshire, England by their liege lord, William the Conqueror, for their aid in the Battle of Hastings.

Asbury . . .

A name which appears frequently in the Beaver family across generations and branches is Asbury. The precise origin of this name within the family is not yet known, but its etymology is clearly understood. Asbury is an English locational name, derived from a place in Cheshire known as Astbury. The name first appears in 12th century records as "Esteburi" and "Asteburi", deriving from the Old English word for "east" and the word "burh", meaning a fortified town. It therefore referred to "a fortified town to the east".

Known Beavers with the name Asbury (sometimes spelled "Ashberry") are as follows (in order of birth):

  1. Asbury Beaver (1829 - Unknown)
  2. Wilson Asbury "Squire" Beaver, Sr. (1831 - 1914)
  3. Martin Asbury Beaver (1844 - 1922)
  4. Ellison Asbury Beaver (1850 - 1919)
  5. Wilson Asbury Beaver, Jr. (1861 - 1945)
  6. Edwin Asbury Beaver (1889 - 1974)
  7. James Asbury Beaver (1908 - 1992)
